# Vehicle Endurance Test Report

## Brake System Performance Evaluation – Prototype Vehicle B12

**Document ID:** VE-B12-BRK-REP-004

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)

**Author:** Test Engineering Department

**Date:** 12.03.2026

**Confidentiality:** Internal Use Only


# 1. Overview

This report summarises the results of endurance testing performed on the braking system of prototype vehicle B12. The objective of the test was to evaluate system stability, thermal behaviour, and performance degradation under extended load conditions.

Testing was conducted over a simulated 15,000 km endurance cycle under mixed driving conditions including urban stop-start, highway cruising, and repeated emergency braking events.

The braking system was evaluated for pressure consistency, stopping distance variation, pad wear rate, and thermal fade characteristics.


# 2. Test Objective

The objective of this test is to assess the long-term performance stability of the hydraulic brake system under repeated thermal and mechanical stress.

Specific aims include:

* Evaluate braking force consistency over endurance cycle
* Measure brake pad wear progression
* Assess thermal fade behaviour under repeated braking events
* Identify any performance drift or degradation patterns


# 3. Test Setup

## 3.1 Vehicle Configuration

* Prototype: B12
* Brake system: Hydraulic disc brake (front and rear)
* ABS: Enabled (standard calibration)
* Tyres: Standard test compound (all-season)

## 3.2 Instrumentation

The following sensors were used:

* Wheel speed sensors
* Brake pressure sensors (front and rear circuits)
* Temperature sensors at caliper and disc surfaces
* Accelerometers for deceleration measurement

## 3.3 Test Environment

* Temperature range: 5°C – 35°C
* Road simulation: Proving ground + test bench cycles
* Humidity: 40–70%


# 4. Test Procedure

The endurance cycle consisted of:

1. Urban driving simulation (frequent stops)
2. Highway steady-state driving
3. Emergency braking events (60 km/h → 0 km/h)
4. Thermal recovery intervals

Each cycle was repeated continuously until total simulated mileage reached 15,000 km.

Brake performance measurements were recorded every 500 km equivalent.


# 5. Results

## 5.1 Braking Performance

Initial and final stopping distances showed a gradual increase over the test period.

* Initial average stopping distance (60 km/h → 0): 34.2 m
* Final average stopping distance: 36.8 m

Observed increase: 7.6%


## 5.2 Brake Pad Wear

Brake pad thickness reduction observed:

* Initial front pad thickness: 11.0 mm

* Final front pad thickness: 8.4 mm

* Initial rear pad thickness: 10.5 mm

* Final rear pad thickness: 9.1 mm

Front axle showed higher wear rate as expected.


## 5.3 Thermal Behaviour

Peak disc temperatures recorded during emergency braking:

* Initial phase: 280°C
* Mid-cycle: 310°C
* Final phase: 335°C

Thermal fade effects became more pronounced after 10,000 km equivalent.


## 5.4 Brake Pressure Consistency

Slight variability observed in hydraulic pressure response under repeated braking:

* Pressure deviation range increased from ±2.1% to ±4.8% over test duration


# 6. Observations

* Gradual degradation in braking performance observed over endurance cycle
* Thermal accumulation impacts consistent braking response after repeated high-load events
* Front brake system shows expected higher wear compared to rear system
* No critical system failures or safety-critical degradation observed


# 7. Conclusion

The brake system of prototype vehicle B12 demonstrates acceptable performance stability over a simulated 15,000 km endurance cycle.

While minor performance degradation and increased thermal sensitivity were observed, these remain within acceptable engineering tolerances for prototype validation testing.

Further optimisation may be required in thermal dissipation characteristics to improve long-term braking consistency under high-load conditions.


# 8. Recommendations

* Investigate improved thermal management for front brake assembly
* Review brake pad compound for wear optimisation
* Conduct extended testing beyond 20,000 km equivalent
* Evaluate alternative brake fluid with higher thermal stability
